# Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before approving ecommerce detail-page output.

## 1. Page structure

- Page count matches requested count or justified adaptation.
- Each page has a distinct role.
- Page order follows a conversion path.
- No page repeats the same information without adding value.
- The final page provides a clear closing purchase reason.

## 2. Product accuracy

- Product name and category are correct.
- Product is visible and recognizable.
- Product shape, package, label, color, and key features remain consistent across pages.
- No invented SKU, origin, certification, test result, function, price, or brand fact appears.
- If a user uploaded a product image, the result preserves identifying features.

## 3. Compliance

- No disease, treatment, prevention, or physiological improvement claim appears for ordinary ecommerce products.
- No absolute words or unsupported rankings appear.
- No fake certification badge, award medal, doctor endorsement, institutional endorsement, or test seal appears.
- High-risk categories use lifestyle language instead of efficacy language.
- Visuals do not imply medical use unless the product and user-provided evidence support it.

## 4. Copy quality

- Main title is concise and memorable.
- Subtitle clarifies benefit or scene.
- Selling points are short enough for mobile scanning.
- Supporting copy is not generic filler.
- Text density is suitable for a mobile detail page.
- Copy tone matches the target audience.
- Chinese copy is accurate, with no typos, OCR mismatch, or garbled characters.

## 5. Visual quality

- Product is the visual center or a clear focal point.
- Lighting supports product texture and commercial appeal.
- Background supports rather than competes with the product.
- Color palette is consistent and appropriate to audience.
- Decorative elements add rhythm without clutter.
- No watermark, random logo, irrelevant object, distorted face/hand, or deformed product appears.

## 6. Layout and mobile readability

- Safe margins are respected.
- Text does not overlap key product areas.
- Text hierarchy is clear: title, subtitle, selling points, body, CTA.
- Important information is readable at phone-screen size.
- Each page has enough breathing room.

## 7. Multi-page consistency

- Style anchor is consistent across all pages.
- Palette, typography mood, icon style, badge style, and decoration language remain stable.
- Product scale and product treatment are compatible across pages.
- Scene changes feel intentional, not random.

## 8. Retry decision rules

Retry a page when:

- product is unclear, missing, distorted, or inconsistent
- page goal is not represented
- compliance risk appears
- text is garbled or incorrect and cannot be fixed by overlay
- background is cluttered or template-like
- visual style drifts from the set
- layout space is unsuitable for the required copy

Use layout fix instead of image retry when:

- background and product are good but text position is wrong
- typo exists only in overlay text
- spacing, alignment, or block hierarchy needs adjustment

Retry the full set only when:

- the visual system is fundamentally wrong
- product identity is not preserved globally
- the page strategy is wrong for the category
- compliance constraints were ignored across multiple pages

## 9. Suggested scoring

- 90-100: production-ready or minor edits only
- 80-89: usable with small layout/copy fixes
- 70-79: retry selected pages
- below 70: major revision or new visual system

Severity guide:

- critical: legal/compliance risk, false claim, wrong product, unusable image
- high: product distortion, illegible key text, major style drift
- medium: weak hierarchy, minor clutter, copy could be sharper
- low: small polish issue


## 10. Conversion QC

Before approving the output, score whether the page sells, not only whether it looks good.

Check:

- First screen clarity: Can the buyer understand product identity and main benefit within 3 seconds?
- Traffic match: Does the first page match the search keyword, ad, short video, live-room hook, or social note promise?
- First three pages: Do they answer product identity, relevance, and core purchase reason?
- One screen, one message: Does each page have one dominant message?
- Buyer question coverage: Does every important buyer question have a page or module?
- Objection handling: Are wrong-purchase anxieties addressed through specs, size, compatibility, usage, care, service, or proof?
- Evidence strength: Are strong claims backed by evidence or softened?
- Visual proof: Do visuals support claims rather than merely decorate the page?
- Trust density: Is trust sufficient without fake badges or invented authority?
- CTA clarity: Does the final page give a clear, safe, non-aggressive purchase reason?

## 11. Conversion failure triggers

Retry or revise when:

- The cover page is attractive but unclear about what the product is.
- The first three pages do not communicate the core purchase reason.
- Multiple pages repeat the same selling point.
- A page is beautiful but does not answer a buyer question.
- A strong claim has no evidence.
- The product appears only as decoration and not as proof.
- The page order jumps from emotion to specs without value explanation.
- The platform style is mismatched, such as a hard-discount Pinduoduo tone for Xiaohongshu seed content.
- The visual system is consistent but the conversion path is weak.

## 12. Scoring update

Use these score bands for conversion QC:

- 90-100: strong conversion path; only minor polish needed.
- 80-89: usable; strengthen one or two weak buyer questions or evidence modules.
- 70-79: revise page sequence or several pages.
- 60-69: weak conversion path; rebuild buyer decision map and page strategy.
- below 60: not production-ready; restart from platform adapter and claim-evidence map.
