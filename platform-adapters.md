# Platform Adapters

Use this reference after brief intake and before buyer decision mapping. If the user does not specify a platform, infer a conservative mobile ecommerce default and mark it as an assumption.

## Universal mobile ecommerce rules

- Design for fast vertical scanning.
- Use one primary message per screen.
- Keep page text short enough for phone viewing.
- Put exact Chinese copy in layout specs rather than inside generated images.
- Match the first page to the traffic source: search keyword, short video hook, live-room promise, ad creative, or social seed note.
- Use platform style as a constraint, not as a reason to invent facts.

## Taobao / Tmall

Best for: brand/store detail pages, longer mobile product explanation, comparison, specs, trust modules, and SKU clarity.

Strategy:
- Balance brand tone, product value, details, specifications, and service.
- Use polished product visuals with moderate information density.
- Include SKU, size, package, materials, care, compatibility, and service modules when relevant.
- Use stronger trust modules only when supported by user-provided proof.

Tone:
- Clear, polished, benefit-oriented, not too aggressive.

Common modules:
- hero visual, core selling points, pain point, detail proof, scenario, usage steps, specifications, service, FAQ, closing CTA.

Avoid:
- fake certification badges, fake store rankings, exaggerated sales claims, cluttered cheap templates.

## Pinduoduo

Best for: direct value communication, price-value, fast choice, offer, bundle, practical proof.

Strategy:
- Strong first-screen benefit and simple value proposition.
- One screen, one selling point.
- Use concise copy, bold hierarchy, offer clarity, package/bundle clarity, and practical scenario proof.
- Add buyer-show, quality report, guarantee, or service only when provided.

Tone:
- Direct, simple, value-focused, practical.

Common modules:
- hero offer, core value, practical pain point, 3-5 benefits, package amount, use scenario, specs, service/offer close.

Avoid:
- over-designed lifestyle pages that hide price-value, complicated brand storytelling, unsupported comparison.

## Douyin ecommerce

Best for: traffic from short video/live, emotional hook, fast visual impact, quick trust, fast action.

Strategy:
- Match the video/live promise in the first page.
- Use large product visuals, strong contrast, short titles, and scenario emotion.
- Convert video script or live-room hook into page sequence if available.
- Prioritize rhythm: hook, why useful, visible proof, how to use, what you get, buy reason.

Tone:
- Punchy, vivid, scene-based, short.

Common modules:
- scroll-stopping hero, pain/scenario, 3-point value, visual proof, usage, package, live-room value/offer, action close.

Avoid:
- dense parameter pages too early, long body copy, text-heavy generated images.

## Xiaohongshu

Best for: seed content, lifestyle product storytelling, aesthetic trust, low-ad feeling, usage experience.

Strategy:
- Make the page feel like a curated recommendation rather than a hard-sell poster.
- Emphasize scene, texture, routine, personal taste, before-use expectation, and everyday integration.
- Keep claims soft and experience-based.

Tone:
- Natural, tasteful, lifestyle-oriented, less aggressive.

Common modules:
- mood hero, scenario problem, experience highlights, texture/detail, how I use it, lifestyle pairing, specs, soft close.

Avoid:
- loud discount style, fake testimonials, excessive badge walls, hard direct-response language.

## Amazon A+ / cross-border

Best for: structured modules, brand story, lifestyle imagery, feature explanations, comparison charts, specifications.

Strategy:
- Use modular content with clear headings and strong image/text balance.
- Include lifestyle images, feature highlights, comparison chart, size/spec module, and brand story when applicable.
- Use claims that are substantiated and internationally understandable.

Tone:
- Clear, benefit-led, credible, structured.

Common modules:
- hero, lifestyle use, feature tiles, how it works, comparison chart, specifications, package contents, brand story, FAQ.

Avoid:
- unsupported superlatives, keyword stuffing in page copy, unverifiable certifications, culturally confusing idioms.

## DTC / Shopify

Best for: brand landing pages, product storytelling, bundles, offer stack, guarantees, reviews, FAQs.

Strategy:
- Use a landing-page-like flow: hero, problem, solution, proof, how it works, social proof, offer, FAQ, guarantee.
- Strongly separate claims from proof and keep evidence visible.

Tone:
- Brand-led, persuasive, story-driven, conversion-oriented.

Common modules:
- hero, problem/solution, benefits, mechanism, lifestyle proof, reviews if provided, bundle, guarantee, FAQ, CTA.

Avoid:
- ecommerce-platform clutter, overlong specs before value is clear, unsupported social proof.

## Traffic source adaptation

- Search traffic: prioritize exact product identity, specs, value, comparison, and SKU clarity.
- Recommendation feed: prioritize visual hook, scene, emotional relevance, and quick benefit.
- Short video/live traffic: mirror the hook, promise, offer, and visual language of the video/live room.
- Social note traffic: keep tone natural, experience-led, and less transactional.
- Paid ad traffic: ensure first page confirms the ad promise and reduces bounce.
- Private domain traffic: emphasize trust, offer, service, and repurchase reason.
